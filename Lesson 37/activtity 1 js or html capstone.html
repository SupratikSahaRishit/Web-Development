Cricket


Cricket emphasizes the "Spirit of the Game", requiring players to compete with honesty, respect opponents and officials, and uphold the highest standards of integrity on and off the field, even when not explicitly bound by the rules.

Cricket Informationt

cricket


Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport for two teams of 11 players, where one team bats to score runs and the other bowls and fields to get batsmen out, played on a central pitch with wickets at each end, with popular formats including multi-day Test matches and limited-overs games like One-Day Internationals (ODIs) and Twenty20 (T20). The objective is to score more runs than the opponent by hitting the ball and running between wickets or hitting boundaries, while the fielding team tries to dismiss batsmen by hitting the wicket, catching the ball, or getting them out in other ways.

Steps of Cricket


To play cricket, set up a pitch with wickets, toss a coin to decide who bats/fields, then the batting team sends two players (striker & non-striker) to hit the ball bowled by the fielding team's bowler, aiming to score runs by running between wickets or hitting boundaries (4s/6s) while the fielders try to get them "out" (bowled, caught, run out, etc.) until 10 outs are made or overs finish, then switch roles, with the higher score winning.
