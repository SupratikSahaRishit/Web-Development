Float On Charles Babbage

Charles Babbage (1791-1871) was an English mathematician and inventor, considered the "father of computing," who designed the first mechanical computers: the Difference Engine and the Analytical Engine. A polymath, Babbage also made contributions to philosophy and other sciences. He was intelligent and ambitious but also known for having a complex, difficult, and somewhat eccentric personality, which, along with his failure to secure sufficient funding, prevented him from completing his ambitious calculating machines during his lifetime.
